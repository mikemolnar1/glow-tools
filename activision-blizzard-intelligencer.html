Walk In Ready

Meeting Prep — Questions That Open Doors

You said you don't know what you're discussing yet. Good — that means the goal of this meeting is intelligence-gathering and relationship-building, not pitching. These questions do that while demonstrating you know their world.

Open with Curiosity
"You've built some of the most iconic gaming campaigns out there — the Zillow integration, the Reven Pack, Ballad of the Blood. How do you decide when to go theatrical vs. cultural embedding vs. community-led?"
Why it works: Shows you've done homework. Gets him talking about his creative philosophy. You learn how he makes decisions.
Explore the Opportunity
"With Monica Austin coming in as CMO, are you going through a period of reassessing how you think about creative partners? Or is the team pretty set?"
Why it works: Acknowledges the leadership change without being weird about it. Directly surfaces whether there's an agency evaluation happening.
Surface the Fandom Angle
"The thing that strikes us about Blizzard is the depth of fandom — WoW players who've been with you for 20 years, the Diablo community that came back after the Vessel of Hatred launch. How are you thinking about deepening that fandom vs. acquiring new players?"
Why it works: Gets him to articulate his current strategic priority. "Deepening fandom" is Glow's lane. "Acquiring new players" is performance marketing. You want to know which he's focused on.
Understand the Full Scope
"You oversee three very different franchises — WoW, Overwatch, Diablo. Each has its own audience and tone. Is there one you're spending the most energy on right now, or is it balanced across all three?"
Why it works: Lets you understand budget/attention priority and figure out where the real opportunity is. Also shows you understand the nuance between franchises.
Hint at the Bridge
"We do a lot of work in sports — we've built our NBA account to over a million a season — and the crossover between sports fandom and gaming fandom is something we think about a lot. Does that overlap matter to how you think about reaching Overwatch's audience?"
Why it works: Name-drops the NBA cred naturally. Opens the sports/gaming bridge conversation without it feeling like a pitch.
